Essay:

Minyanville's World in Review with Hoofy & Boo is the world's first (and only) animated business news program.

Each weekly report of Minyanville's World in Review with Hoofy & Boo takes a look at a relevant financial topic and delivers it in a way that is smart, meaningful, and entertaining.  The gap between what people need to know about their finances and what they actually do know has never been greater. The challenge with bridging the gap in financial understanding is providing content that is not only informative, but also entertaining—which is why we've chosen animation as a creative technique.

Animation is a tremendous equalizer. The genre is now not only an accepted form, but in the new media landscape, a respected one.  Business and financial news used to be the province of a few.  Wall Street and Main Street rarely intersected.  With Minyanville's World in Review with Hoofy & Boo, this is no longer the case.

People are very emotionally connected to their money, but also very intimidated by the thought of managing it. The growing number of financial websites, television networks, and "instant experts" has made it difficult to know what's important. Minyanville's World in Review with Hoofy & Boo connects with people viscerally through humor and insight.  The show is Emmy-worthy because it raises the level of financial understanding while reducing that intimidation factor.

EPISODE GUIDE

1.) Societe Generale's Trading Debacle

     Airdate: 2/15/2008

In this report, Hoofy and Boo took a look at Societe Generale, the French bank rocked by a massive fraud perpetrated by rogue trader Jerome Kerviel in January, 2008.  The goal of this piece was to explore how Kerviel managed to lose the bank over $7 billion, and how the fraud went undetected for so long.  The importance of this piece was to explain the scandal's fundamental causes, fallout, and provide a look at the major players involved.

 Hoofy and Boo's report on Societe Generale impacted audiences by outlining the cause of the bank's loss and raised awareness that even in a world of heightened risk management and increased checks and balances, nothing is too big to fail and lapses in oversight can prove to be catastrophic.

 2.) Is the Hummer Dead Weight to GM?

     Airdate: 8/29/2008

 The goal of this particular piece was to examine possible financial alternative available to General Motors' as it tries to stem its losses, specifically selling the Hummer Brand. The importance of this piece was to take a look at how that strategy would affect both GM's bottom line, as well as its place in the hierarchy of global auto manufacturers. 

 Hoofy and Boo's report on GM's Hummer dilemma impacted audiences by pointing out that by selling the brand, GM would be shedding more than a money losing car line; it would be moving away from both a business philosophy as well as a position of leadership it has enjoyed since its inception. 

3.) Oil: Supply and Demand

     Airdate: 1/30/2008

 The goal of this particular piece was to break down the complexities of worldwide oil supply and demand. This piece was important because it clearly explained the disparity between global oil supply and demand, and how it has created a long term problem for which short term solutions will no longer suffice.

 Hoofy and Boo's report on oil impacted audiences by clearly and simply showing that the problems of oil supply and demand were not limited to the U.S., but were part of a global issue containing both energy and geopolitical implications that were not going away, despite the daily fluctuations of crude.

4.) Should Starbucks' Expansion Make Investors Jittery?

     Airdate: 8/1/2007

 The goal of this particular piece was to examine Starbucks' aggressive expansion strategy in a simple and straightforward way. The importance of the piece was to

point out the fact that the company's plan might be flawed, would be difficult for them to sustain, and could prove costly to investors—which eventually proved true.

 The most important impact of Hoofy & Boo's News & Views's report on Starbucks was to make credible and useful investment information accessible to more people at a time when it was most critical.

5.) The Mismanagement of Countrywide

     Airdate: 12/14/2007

 The goal of this particular piece was to point out how Countrywide CEO Angelo Mozilo might not have been fully engaged in the daily management of the company, and the implications that it had to shareholders.  The importance of the piece was to remind investors how crucial a company's management is to a stock's performance.

 Hoofy and Boo's report on the management of Countrywide impacted audiences by pointing out that the sound management practices are crucial to companies everywhere, and that neglecting one's role as a hands-on, engaged manager could have significant consequences to investors. 

6.) Alternative Investment Strategies: The Vice Fund

     Airdate: 9/5/2007

 The goal of this particular piece was to inform viewers on one of the more unusual ways to invest in mutual funds: "vice funds". More people than ever are investing in socially-responsible companies. The importance of this piece was to show that in many cases alternative investment strategies actually outperform their straight-laced counterparts. 

The most important impact of Hoofy and Boo'sreport on vice funds was to shed light on an alternative investment that many people did not know about.
